## 1. Project Goal
The goal of the "Flower Shop" project is to develop a modern, full-stack web application that solves the usability crisis in the current online floral market by leveraging advanced **Object-Oriented Programming (OOP)** principles and **Graph Theory algorithms**.

## 2. The Market Problem: Why Current Solutions Fail

We conducted an analysis of standard online flower delivery services (local shops, typical CMS-based sites) and identified critical flaws in the user experience (UX):

* **Obsolete UI/UX:** Most existing sites are built on outdated legacy platforms (older WordPress/OpenCart themes). They are cluttered, visually unappealing, and non-responsive.
* **High User Friction:** The purchasing process is often counter-intuitive. Users are bombarded with distracting banners and poor navigation, killing the desire to make a purchase.
* **Lack of Engagement:** Flowers are an emotional product, but current sites act merely as static catalogs. They do not allow users to interact with the product or customize it meaningfully.
* **"Black Box" Pricing:** Users rarely understand why a custom arrangement costs a specific amount, leading to mistrust and abandoned carts.

**Conclusion:** The poor technical implementation of current market solutions directly leads to low conversion rates and customer dissatisfaction.

## 3. Our Solution: A Tech-Driven User Experience

The "Flower Shop" project addresses these issues by treating the application as a piece of software engineering, not just a website.
